I wanted to see my sister that I had not seen in over a year while she lived in California. I thought about flying out to see her but my sister and parent's had a different idea. She drove a Jeep Wrangler with a soft top and was starting to not feel safe alone in that car. Rather than get a new car, my parents suggested that I drive out to San Jose California from New Jersey in order to swap cars with her.

Not wanting to go alone, I invited my boyfriend to come with me. However, both of the cars that I needed to drive were manual transmissions. My boyfriend does not know how to drive stick, so I drove the entire way. The trip out to California and back took us 10 days total while camping different spots at night. I drove over 12 hours most days and on one of those days, I actually drove over 1,000 miles from the North West entrance of Yellowstone National Park to San Jose, California.

After the long days of driving to California, we had only a 2 day visit with my sister in order to make it back to New Jersey in time for the semester to start. On the way back, I drove the manual Jeep Wrangler which was horrible against the winds. The trip was a great time, and we got to see many different sites around the USA including the Grand Canyon. I cannot wait to do another cross-country road trip, but hopefully I will have more time to get there and will not be the only one driving.
